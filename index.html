I wanted to test whether scarifying milkweed seeds helps them germinate faster. I used swamp milkweed and butterfly weed seeds. Scarification was done by cutting off the tip of each seed.

  • Two sets of seeds were used: scarified and non-scarified.

  • Both cups were floated in a larger container filled with the same water (room temp rainwater).
  • The setup was placed outside in the shade.
  • No pre-cold stratification was used.

After 12 hours: Scarified swamp milkweed seeds already showed a white root tip (radicle) starting to emerge. Non-scarified seeds showed nothing.

After 24 hours: Almost all scarified seeds had begun germinating. Out of 8 seeds, 7 had visible growth. Non-scarified seeds remained unchanged.

For butterfly weed, scarified seeds showed tiny white dots after 24 hours, while the non-scarified ones still showed no sign of activity.

üåø Conclusion

Scarifying milkweed seeds clearly accelerated germination without needing cold stratification. This result is surprising, especially since no pre-chilling was done, yet rapid germination occurred just in water, outdoors, in South Florida.

This experiment suggests scarification can be a useful alternative or addition to stratification for milkweed seed starting in warm climates.
